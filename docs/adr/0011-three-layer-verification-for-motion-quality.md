# Motion/animation quality is verified with a three-layer strategy: pose-stream math invariants + e2e behavioral checks + one authoritative video per feel-PR

Building Demo Mode's Canyon-tour flight (#91) exposed that our existing e2e (Playwright) suite — which asserts *behavior* ("the toggle flies the camera", "a Panel is visible") — **cannot certify motion *quality***. Green e2e runs shipped a near-vertical "elevator" climb, instant roll snaps, a mid-flight stutter, and ~160 rad/s aim spins; none tripped a behavioral assertion. Conversely, using a full headless **video capture as the iteration loop** cost ~20 min per change and leaked kind clusters / app servers (see [[worktree-and-resource-cleanup]]). We therefore verify motion/animation-quality work in **three layers, each doing what only it can**.

## The three layers

1. **Pose-stream math invariants** (vitest, renderer-free, ~seconds). Sample the pure motion seam (`createDemoTour`/`stepDemoTour`/`sampleDemoTourPose`, `demoPose`) over multiple seeds at a fixed frame rate and assert per-frame **continuity/quality bounds**: bounded roll rate & angular acceleration, roll≈0 on straight flight, ground-speed continuity, aim yaw/pitch-rate caps, void clearance, climb-gradient, determinism. This is the **inner loop and the permanent regression guard** for feel. It caught four motion bugs the green e2e never saw. Runs on every PR (`web/src/scene/demoModeSmoothness.test.ts` is the first instance).
2. **Targeted e2e behavioral checks** (Playwright, minutes). The real toggle drives the real *built binary* against a real test cluster (per [[0004-two-tier-test-cluster-strategy]]): hand-off has no teleport, canyon-low/overview-high/perimeter passes actually render, screenshots are framed. This is the **"the app genuinely works and didn't regress"** confidence. Every PR touching the flight.
3. **One authoritative video capture per feel-changing PR** (frontend-tester). Choreography and aesthetics are irreducibly human judgments (see [[0003-cinematic-viewer-not-admin-tool]]); the capture is a **merge gate and the artifact the maintainer reviews**, not an iteration loop. `task capture:record` (`test/e2e/capture/`, issue #120) is the maintained tool for this: raw CDP `Page.startScreencast` capture (not Playwright's built-in recorder, whose preset destroys the micro-motion detail this layer exists to judge) plus offline exact-speed encode and pose-trace analysis (strongest turns, yaw-rate saturation clusters, tower proximity, labeled stills) — see `docs/running-locally.md` for the full recipe and rationale.

## Why, and the anti-patterns it forbids

- **Speed where it matters, confidence where it counts.** Layer 1 gives fast, mechanical, deterministic feedback on the thing hardest to eyeball reliably (frame-to-frame continuity); layer 2 proves the wired-up app behaves; layer 3 is the human's call on *feel*.
- **Do not use layer 3 as layer 1.** A 20-min video loop per tweak stalls iteration and leaks resources. Iterate against layer 1; capture video once, at the end.
- **Do not trust layer 2 to certify feel.** A passing `toBeVisible` proved nothing about an 8-second roll snap. Behavioral green ≠ smooth.

## Amendment (#105 iterations 1-4): four rules for building and calibrating layer 1 itself

Iteration 1 is PR #116. Iterations 2 and 3 both landed in PR #118 (iteration 3 a same-PR follow-up round after the maintainer's video verdict on iteration 2, not a separate PR). Iteration 4 is issue #121, delivered by PR #126. Four rules for the *building-and-calibrating* loop — distinct from the motion bugs the invariants themselves catch — came out of these threads. The bounds stay in `web/src/scene/demoModeSmoothness.test.ts` (the living catalogue); this section is the method only.

- **Build the metric before tuning.** Iteration 2 (#118) won on every existing bound and regressed framing, which nothing measured. Iteration 3 added `maxVoidFraction`/`minCanyonFraction`; iteration 4 (#121) hit the same trap on a new axis and built aim-pitch and vantage-variety metrics against `main` *before* any fix — finding 0.48-0.60 of every tour's frames pitched up >5°, and kwok7's worst seed at exactly 0.000 wide-vantage share.
- **Construction guards vs. tuning guards.** A construction guard is exact and mechanism-derived (the view-yaw acceleration bound just restates `VIEW_YAW_MAX_ACCEL`, the follower's own cap). A tuning guard is calibrated, and must be bracketed between the current observation and the known-bad value it excludes — never fitted to current behaviour, which guards nothing.
- **State the live observation in the bound's comment.** #126's review round 1 caught the horizon side-change bound's comment still claiming "observed ≤12/min" after a later change pushed the real worst to 15.3/min against the unchanged 16/min bound — 1.046x headroom, not the ~1.3x implied; a prior edit had silently no-opped the update. #118's review round 1 separately caught a saturation bound relaxed to 0.20, *above* the ~18% known-bad regime the same comment cited.
- **Pin coupled constants with a pair-guard.** `CORNER_TURN_RADIUS > CORNER_ARC_TARGET_CHORD` is pinned directly (the corner geometry degenerates at equality). A second pair-guard, landed with #126 (`web/src/scene/demoMode.test.ts`), pins the wide-phase start waypoint against the climb lead-in and was itself found neutered in review — a `Math.floor` made its lower half unreachable — fixed to `Math.ceil`.

The human's complaint is the specification: every bound in the smoothness suite exists because a maintainer described a feeling in plain language — "It gives a feeling of the captain always cranking his head up," "The current state gives a cramped/narrow view the whole time" (#121) — and it became a number checked against a real pose stream. That loop is why layer 3 stays a merge gate.

## Scope

Applies to Demo Mode and future camera/animation work (Focus, Demo Mode refinements, any procedural motion). Ordinary non-animated frontend changes need only the usual unit + e2e coverage; the layer-1 pose-stream harness is specifically for *motion-quality* work.
