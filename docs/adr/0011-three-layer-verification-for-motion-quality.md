# Motion/animation quality is verified with a three-layer strategy: pose-stream math invariants + e2e behavioral checks + one authoritative video per feel-PR

Building Demo Mode's Canyon-tour flight (#91) exposed that our existing e2e (Playwright) suite — which asserts *behavior* ("the toggle flies the camera", "a Panel is visible") — **cannot certify motion *quality***. Green e2e runs shipped a near-vertical "elevator" climb, instant roll snaps, a mid-flight stutter, and ~160 rad/s aim spins; none tripped a behavioral assertion. Conversely, using a full headless **video capture as the iteration loop** cost ~20 min per change and leaked kind clusters / app servers (see [[worktree-and-resource-cleanup]]). We therefore verify motion/animation-quality work in **three layers, each doing what only it can**.

## The three layers

1. **Pose-stream math invariants** (vitest, renderer-free, ~seconds). Sample the pure motion seam (`createDemoTour`/`stepDemoTour`/`sampleDemoTourPose`, `demoPose`) over multiple seeds at a fixed frame rate and assert per-frame **continuity/quality bounds**: bounded roll rate & angular acceleration, roll≈0 on straight flight, ground-speed continuity, aim yaw/pitch-rate caps, void clearance, climb-gradient, determinism. This is the **inner loop and the permanent regression guard** for feel. It caught four motion bugs the green e2e never saw. Runs on every PR (`web/src/scene/demoModeSmoothness.test.ts` is the first instance).
2. **Targeted e2e behavioral checks** (Playwright, minutes). The real toggle drives the real *built binary* against a real test cluster (per [[0004-two-tier-test-cluster-strategy]]): hand-off has no teleport, canyon-low/overview-high/perimeter passes actually render, screenshots are framed. This is the **"the app genuinely works and didn't regress"** confidence. Every PR touching the flight.
3. **One authoritative video capture per feel-changing PR** (frontend-tester). Choreography and aesthetics are irreducibly human judgments (see [[0003-cinematic-viewer-not-admin-tool]]); the capture is a **merge gate and the artifact the maintainer reviews**, not an iteration loop. `task capture:record` (`test/e2e/capture/`, issue #120) is the maintained tool for this: raw CDP `Page.startScreencast` capture (not Playwright's built-in recorder, whose preset destroys the micro-motion detail this layer exists to judge) plus offline exact-speed encode and pose-trace analysis (strongest turns, yaw-rate saturation clusters, tower proximity, labeled stills) — see `docs/running-locally.md` for the full recipe and rationale.

## Why, and the anti-patterns it forbids

- **Speed where it matters, confidence where it counts.** Layer 1 gives fast, mechanical, deterministic feedback on the thing hardest to eyeball reliably (frame-to-frame continuity); layer 2 proves the wired-up app behaves; layer 3 is the human's call on *feel*.
- **Do not use layer 3 as layer 1.** A 20-min video loop per tweak stalls iteration and leaks resources. Iterate against layer 1; capture video once, at the end.
- **Do not trust layer 2 to certify feel.** A passing `toBeVisible` proved nothing about an 8-second roll snap. Behavioral green ≠ smooth.

## Amendment (#105 iterations 1-4 / PRs #116, #118, #126): four rules for building and calibrating layer 1 itself, each earned by a real failure

Layer 1 is not just a fixed catalogue of invariants — it's a loop of building metrics and calibrating their bounds, and that loop has its own failure modes, distinct from the motion bugs it exists to catch. Four rules came out of the #105 feel iterations. The bounds themselves stay in `web/src/scene/demoModeSmoothness.test.ts` (the living catalogue — this amendment does not restate them, and would go stale if it tried); what belongs here is the method, because it generalizes to any future motion-quality metric.

1. **A metric that doesn't exist is a regression nobody can see — build it before tuning.** Iteration 2 (#118) optimized hard against every smoothness bound that existed (roll, view-follower rate/accel, ground speed) and won on all of them, then regressed *framing* — nothing measured whether a Tower was even in frame. The maintainer's layer-3 video caught it after hours of green layer-1 runs: wide, gentle corners read as lost dynamism and more black frame. Iteration 3 closed that gap with `maxVoidFraction`/`minCanyonFraction`, and iteration 4 (#121, PR #126) hit the *same trap on a new axis*: aim pitch and vantage variety were still unmeasured. Per the ticket's now-explicit rule, iteration 4's own first move was building those two metrics and running them against `main` *before touching any code* — which is how it found, in numbers, that more than half of every tour's frames pitched the gaze up and one grid's wide-vantage share was exactly zero. When a feel complaint names an axis nothing measures, the first work is instrumenting that axis, not adjusting bounds that already have coverage.
2. **Two kinds of bounds — construction guards and tuning guards — and a tuning guard must be bracketed, never fitted.** A *construction* guard is exact because the implementation enforces it by mechanism (the view-yaw acceleration invariant just re-asserts `VIEW_YAW_MAX_ACCEL`, the follower's own cap — a violation means the follower itself broke, not that tuning drifted). A *tuning* guard is calibrated, and is only honest when bracketed **above the current observation and below the known-bad value it exists to keep out** — never fitted to whatever the current build happens to produce, which guards nothing (the next commit's "current" is by definition inside a bound fitted to it). The horizon side-change guard is the running example: pinned below the pre-#105 16.7-25.3/min flip-flop regime the maintainer called out as "the horizon flip-flops," and above every seed's worst observed. State which kind a new bound is when you add it.
3. **State the live observation in the bound's comment — a stale one hides the very regression the bound exists to expose.** #126's review round 1 caught the horizon side-change bound's comment still claiming "observed ≤ 12/min" after a widening change had pushed the real worst to 15.3/min against the unchanged 16/min bound — real headroom 1.046x, not the ~1.3x the stale comment implied; an earlier edit had silently no-opped the update. #118's review round 1 separately caught a pan-saturation bound relaxed to 0.20 that sat *above* the ~18%-of-frames known-bad pinning regime the same comment cited as the thing the bound excludes. Both were caught in review, not by CI — a bound's comment is part of the guard, and a wrong one is worse than no comment.
4. **Pin coupled constants with a pair-guard in the suite.** Where two constants must stay in a specific relationship or a mechanism degenerates, assert that relationship directly — don't trust each constant's own bound to keep them apart. The corner-rounding geometry collapses exactly where `CORNER_TURN_RADIUS` meets `CORNER_ARC_TARGET_CHORD` (measured: at equality the spline wiggle returns and the horizon side-change rate triples), so the suite pins `CORNER_TURN_RADIUS > CORNER_ARC_TARGET_CHORD` directly rather than relying on each constant's own bound. A second pair-guard, added for the wide-overview-vantage work (`web/src/scene/demoMode.test.ts`, #126), pins the wide phase's start waypoint at or after the climb's lead-in — and was itself found neutered on first review: the lead-in derivation used `Math.floor`, which rounded down to a floor the constant's current value would have cleared even from inside the range the guard's own comment claimed to forbid; `Math.ceil` made the derivation exact.

Underlying all four: **the human's complaint is the specification.** Every bound in this file exists because a maintainer described something in plain language — "the horizon flip-flops," "it feels cramped," "the captain is always cranking his head up" — and it was turned into a number checked against a real pose stream. That plain-language-to-number loop is why layer 3 stays a merge gate rather than a formality: it's where the next complaint comes from.

## Scope

Applies to Demo Mode and future camera/animation work (Focus, Demo Mode refinements, any procedural motion). Ordinary non-animated frontend changes need only the usual unit + e2e coverage; the layer-1 pose-stream harness is specifically for *motion-quality* work.
